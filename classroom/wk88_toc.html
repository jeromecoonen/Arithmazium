The Floating Point Indoctrination

E The Indoctrination is for Enthusiasts.

Prof. William Kahan of the University of California at Berkeley presented 27 lectures during May-July 1988. Formally listed as graduate-level class CS 279 titled Computer System Support for Scientific and Engineering Computation. the course was affectionately known as The Floating Point Indoctrination.

This web page contains a re-creation of this one-time course designed for a combination academic and industry audience. Some 88 students registered. Many attended remotely via VCR recordings. Students took turns publishing notes for the lectures. David Hough, who coordinated the lectures at Sun Microsystems in Mountain View, California, distributed those notes along with over two thousand pages of other handouts.

The three sections below contain the lectures with notes (often including the overhead transparencies), and links to the complete collection of handouts. It's all here!
