Welcome to the Arithmazium!

This site tells a story of computer arithmetic. The target audience is anyone age 5 to 75. Undergraduate students will find useful lore here that is rarely found in any curriculum. Arithmazium strives to be engaging rather than encyclopedic. The site offers bits even the most devoted experts might enjoy.

You can always return to the home page by clicking the blue Arithmazium banner.

Reader's guide

You need no more than enthusiasm to approach Arithmazium. The site presents many stories, not a tsunami of facts. Arithmetic is the subject. You need just elementary mathematics to get a long way down the path.

When optional mathematical content arises to enrich the story, it is highlighted like this. But Arithmazium is self-contained without readers poring over every fantastic formula like \[4/3 = 1 + 1/4 + 1/16 + 1/64 + 1/256 + 1/1024 + \cdots\] so please feel free to skim or skip the math.

Of course, computer arithmetic is exploited through programming, but you won't need sophisticated programming knowledge to follow the thread. Literally, many of the code samples you'll see are variations on a simple theme: 

    new_value = old_value / TWO
The patience to read such formulas and to follow the binary bits or decimal digits where they fall is all you need.

If you're 5 and reading this, you don't need my guidance. If you're with a young person or you miraculously find yourself here without having encountered computer arithmetic before, go to the Playroom, scheduled to open Fall 2020.
