<h1>Precision</h1>

<p>
If the radix <code>B</code> is 1, perhaps because of log
arithmetic, defer the computation to the second look
at precision to come.
Otherwise, call
<a class="inline" href="find_precision_big_B_to_nth.html">
  <code>find_precision_big_B_to_nth()</code></a> to
loop on powers of <code>B</code> until adding \(1\) causes
no increase. The diagram shows the situation in binary as
<code>big_b</code> grows just large enough that an added
\(1\) just rounds off.
</p>

<object type="image/svg+xml" data="img/precision_a-auto.svg">
  ------Image------
</object>

<p>
The computation works as well in radix 8, 10, or 16. In these cases,
adding \(1\) beyond the last digit position should always round off.
</p>

#CODE 735-precision

<p>
In most cases, <code>1.0 / BIG_B_NTH</code> gives
\(1 / B\) of a unit in the last place (ulp) of \(1\).
The values will be refined in code to come.
</p>
