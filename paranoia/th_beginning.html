Welcome to Paranoia: The Program!

This is the story of Prof. William Kahan's BASIC program, developed in the early 1980s on an original IBM PC. Paranoia became an instant cult-classic in the numerical community because of its ability to tease out misbehavior at the edges of computer arithmetic.

The story is worth telling, and may you find it worth reading, because Paranoia has the highest concentration of accessible arithmetic wizardry you'll find anywhere. There is no comparison.

To bring the conversation up to the 21st century, I've recast Paranoia in Python, where we have the luxury of variable names more descriptive than U2 and no limit on commentary. You will need no specific Python knowledge to read this story. Familiarity with any programming language will suffice.

The next pages set some the context, but of course you're welcome to dive in anywhere to get a feel for the code. Paranoia's experiments are designed to be independent, so you can understand what's happening without inhaling the whole body of code.

Welcome, and good luck!
